University of California, Riverside REU

June 2024 - August 2024

From June to August, I had the opportunity to attend an REU at the University of California, Riverside. This experience allowed me to deepen my understanding of data analysis and collection. In the first month, our team brainstormed ideas and developed a 2D model of a city, similar to NYC. The goal? To identify busy areas within the model using real data from Uber and taxi pickups, and then cluster this data to achieve more accurate results. This brought us a step closer to creating the tensor needed for our autonomous vehicle project.

In the following months, we refined our data using various clustering techniques. We proposed using silhouette scoring to finalize the results from the graphs, which highlighted heavy traffic flows. With this foundation, we were finally able to start testing and creating simulations for different scenarios that the autonomous vehicle might encounter.
